**Definition:** The timeframe when fares are most likely to be at their lowest - not too early (premium prices), not too late (desperation pricing), just right.

## üéØ BOOKING WINDOW BEST PRACTICES

### **When to Book by Trip Type:**

**Domestic Flights:**
- Off-peak: 1-3 months ahead
- Peak season (summer, holidays): 3-5 months ahead
- Sweet spot: 43 days before departure average

**International Flights:**
- Off-peak: 2-8 months ahead
- Peak season: 4-10 months ahead
- Spring break specifically: Book end of January to mid-February

### **The "21-Day Rule"**
**Critical Discovery:** Most airline advance purchase requirements kick in 21 days before departure. On Day 20, cheapest fares disappear and prices jump $100-200+.

**For Your Trip:** March 31 departure = 21-day deadline is **March 10**. You have ~3.5 weeks before this cliff.

### **Why Goldilocks Window Works:**

**Too Early (6-12 months out):**
- Airlines charge premium for advance planning convenience
- Fares often higher when seats first released
- Airlines know customers booking this early have fixed plans

**Just Right (Goldilocks Window):**
- Airlines actively managing inventory
- Competitive pricing to fill seats
- Best balance of availability and price

**Too Late (Last 3 weeks):**
- Business travelers booking (price insensitive)
- Limited seat availability
- Desperation pricing by airlines
- Prices spike significantly

## ‚ö†Ô∏è COMMON MISTAKES DISCOVERED

### **Mistake 1: Comparing Base Fares Instead of True Total Cost**
**The Problem:** Budget airlines show low base fare but charge heavily for bags, seats, priority
**Example from research:** 
- Budget airline: ¬£49 base + ¬£80 bags + ¬£24 priority = ¬£153 total
- Full-service: ¬£86 base with bags included = ¬£86 total
- Budget airline actually ¬£67 MORE expensive!

**Lesson:** NEVER compare base fares - always calculate true total with ALL required add-ons

### **Mistake 2: Not Reading Baggage Policies**
**The Problem:** Assuming baggage is included or underestimating fees
**Stats:** U.S. airlines earned $5.3 billion from baggage fees in 2021
**Solution:** Check EACH airline's baggage policy before comparison

### **Mistake 3: Booking Too Late**
**The Problem:** Waiting for "last-minute deals" that rarely exist anymore
**Reality:** Last-minute deals are largely myth - prices rise in final 3 weeks
**Your Situation:** Already at late edge - don't wait for deals

### **Mistake 4: Pre-Paying Baggage at Airport**
**The Problem:** Airport baggage fees up to 50% higher than online pre-payment
**Solution:** ALWAYS pre-pay baggage online during booking

### **Mistake 5: Not Checking True Cost**
**Hidden fees that inflate prices 20-40%:**
- Checked baggage (¬£25-50 per bag per flight)
- Cabin bags (¬£10-25 on budget airlines)
- Seat selection (¬£10-30 per person per flight)
- Priority boarding (¬£6-12 per person per flight)
- Card payment fees (1-3%)
- Airport check-in fees (¬£25-55 if not checked in online)

### **Mistake 6: Assuming All Search Engines Show Same Prices**
**Reality:** Different engines show different prices
**Frommers testing:** Momondo found best prices on 50% of test routes
**Strategy:** Use multiple tools - Google Flights + Skyscanner minimum

### **Mistake 7: Booking Too Early**
**The Problem:** Booking 6-12 months out locks in premium pricing
**Morocco relevance:** Less relevant - you're at opposite extreme (too late not too early)
